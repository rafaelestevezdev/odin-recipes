Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a traditional Roman pasta dish made with eggs, cheese (Pecorino Romano), pancetta or guanciale, and black pepper. It's a rich and comforting meal that comes together quickly, relying on the warmth of the pasta to cook the eggs into a creamy sauce.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100g) Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • Olive oil (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat a large pan over medium heat. Add the pancetta (or guanciale) and cook until it becomes crispy, about 5-7 minutes. If needed, drizzle a bit of olive oil.
  3. In a bowl, whisk the eggs with the grated Pecorino Romano and a good amount of freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Once the pasta is ready, reserve about 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
  5. Add the hot pasta to the pan with the crispy pancetta, stirring to combine. Remove from heat.
  6. Slowly pour the egg and cheese mixture into the pasta, tossing quickly to create a creamy sauce. Add a little pasta water if needed to adjust the sauce consistency.
  7. Serve immediately, topped with more black pepper and Pecorino Romano.
